stellar-classifier ML research

Stellar Classifier

A reproducible study of how far you can trust a photometric classifier of galaxies, stars, and quasars on 100,000 SDSS objects. Five self-contained experiments isolate where accuracy actually comes from and where it breaks down: redshift leakage that inflates results, degradation under injected noise, which photometric bands carry signal, and the generalization gap when training and testing on different brightness regimes. A single notebook restructured into a modular, re-runnable repository.

IEEE URTC Paper · in preparation

Calibration robustness under magnitude shift

Sole-author paper measuring whether photometric star / galaxy / quasar classifiers stay calibrated as sources get fainter, across 499,995 SDSS objects. Capable models stay well calibrated across the magnitude range (expected calibration error around 0.003 to 0.007); the real hazard is naive recalibration, where Platt scaling fit on bright sources roughly quintuples faint-end error while temperature scaling transfers best. Targeting IEEE URTC. In preparation.
